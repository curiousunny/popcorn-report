## What we want to know

*Do children from different backgrounds receive different care when hospitalized for bronchiolitis?*

Bronchiolitis is one of the most common reasons babies and young children end up in hospital. National guidelines recommend simple supportive care, but some hospitals still use tests and treatments that don't help — which can mean some children receive extra or unnecessary care, and longer stays.

We want to find out whether these differences in care are linked to things like a child's race or ethnicity, family income, or the language a family speaks, and how those factors relate to length of stay, intensive care admissions, and other outcomes.

## How we're doing it

*We're reviewing 25 years of published research on bronchiolitis hospitalizations.*

We are reviewing research published since the year 2000 on babies under two years old who were hospitalized with bronchiolitis. We are looking at how often low-value tests or treatments were used across different patient backgrounds.

## Where we are now

*We've narrowed nearly 5,000 studies down to 65 that will shape our findings.*

We have screened nearly 5,000 abstracts, narrowing to 65 full-text studies that will be included in our manuscript. Next, we will extract the data from those 65 studies to synthesize how sociodemographic characteristics and markers of structural inequities may contribute to low-value care in children hospitalized with bronchiolitis.
