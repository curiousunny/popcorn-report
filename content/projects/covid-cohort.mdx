## What we wanted to know

*Does getting COVID-19 or being vaccinated against it have lasting effects on children's health, quality of life, or mental health?*

The short-term and long-term effects of COVID-19 infection and vaccination in children.

## How we did it

*We followed families for a year, checking in several times, and collected blood samples from a smaller group.*

We invited families to join the study when they came to the emergency department or hospital with a COVID-19 infection. We gathered information from their medical charts, and families completed health and quality-of-life questionnaires at four to five points over one year. An optional biobank sub-study collected blood samples from patients with confirmed rare conditions (MIS-C, myocarditis, pericarditis) after vaccination or infection, along with samples from matched controls.

## Where we are now

*Follow-up is complete, the database is closed, and several analyses are in progress.*

Follow-up ended in May 2025, and we have closed the database. The data is now supporting a range of in-progress studies, including predictive models, long-term quality-of-life analysis, and secondary analyses of hospital length of stay across demographic groups.

**What we learned about running the study itself:**

- Keeping families engaged across multiple follow-ups was hard — completion rates dropped at later visits. Flexible contact methods and self-scheduling would help.
- Caregivers asked more questions about the study's purpose at later follow-ups, suggesting we need to reinforce the "why" throughout, not just at enrollment.
- Scheduling conflicts limited biobank sample collection. Less invasive methods (saliva, finger-prick, leftover clinical samples) would improve recruitment.
- Language barriers showed up at several sites — more translated materials and interpretation support are needed.
